Delphi Website Copy — February 2026

Brand Context: Everything You Need

What is Delphi?

Delphi is a Digital Mind — an AI-powered representation of your thinking, voice, and judgment that can answer, guide, and connect in your voice, 24/7. It's not a chatbot or a clone. It's a reflection of your knowledge that lets others learn through conversation.

Who Delphi Is For

  • People whose relationships depend on what they know and how they think
  • CEOs, founders, creators, experts, professors, coaches
  • Anyone with real experience who has repetitive professional conversations
  • People who have others wanting to talk to them but limited time

What Users Should Think/Feel/Know

  • Think: "My intelligence deserves to be shared at scale"
  • Feel: Trust, confidence, excitement — like discovering something magical yet rewarding
  • Know: Their Delphi is a destination — an always-on link that handles repetitive questions so they can focus on what's new and high-value. NOT a takeover of their existing conversations

The User Journey

Where They Are NowWhere We Want Them
They know they're the bottleneck — too many people need their time→They see Delphi as a way to unblock themselves
They know who they talk to and what they get asked a lot→They realize how much of their time actually goes to repetitive conversations
They're wary of AI — worried it's inauthentic, will replace them, or they'll lose control→They see Delphi as an artifact they own — like an always-on personal website that amplifies (not replaces) them
They're worried about being misrepresented by AI tools→They see Delphi as a way to own their narrative
Their content is scattered everywhere (podcasts, articles, videos)→They see Delphi as a way to centralize their knowledge — owned and controlled by them
They think "this is only for influencers having 100s of conversations"→They realize even 5 similar conversations a week adds up — Delphi is for them too
They worry they'll have to read every conversation that comes in→They understand Delphi surfaces the most interesting people and insights — they won't miss important connections

Examples of Repetitive Conversations Delphi Can Handle

The intro call: Explaining your career path, background, and how you got here — every first meeting
The founder story: Why you started the company, the backstory, company culture — repeated to every candidate, investor, journalist
The syllabus questions: Students asking about the exam when it's already in the syllabus, or clarifying assignment details
The "did you see my post" follow-up: Readers asking questions that were answered in a previous article or video

The idea: say it once, and your Delphi can talk about it like you — so you only focus on what's new, high-value, and deep.

Voice & Tone

Intelligent but warm. Thoughtful, conversational, and precise. Empowering — it invites users to own and expand their brilliance. Not technical jargon. Not corporate/salesy. Not AI-speak or mechanical.

DO

  • Center copy around the user's brilliance
  • Call it a "Digital Mind"
  • Emphasize control and ownership
  • Emphasize human elements
  • Make it feel magical but not magic
  • Be accessible most of the time and aspirational only when it doesn't make ICP think this is not for them

DON'T

  • Say "clone" or "replica"
  • Call it automation
  • Use words like "workflow" or "bot"
  • Be overly technical, jargony, corporate, or salesy
  • Focus on vanity over value
